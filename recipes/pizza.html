Peperonni Pizza

Description

Pepperoni pizza is a timeless classic and a staple of Italian-American cuisine. It consists of a fermented dough base topped with a savory tomato sauce, a generous layer of melted mozzarella cheese, and thin slices of pepperoni. Pepperoni is a dry-cured, spicy sausage made from beef and pork, seasoned with paprika and chili flakes. When baked at high temperatures, the pepperoni slices become crispy and release a flavorful oil that seasons the entire pizza.

Ingredients

  • ​500g All-purpose flour or Bread flour
  • ​300ml Warm water
  • 7g Active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of Sugar
  • ​1 teaspoon of Salt
  • 2 tablespoons of Olive oil
  • 1/2 cup of Tomato sauce (Pizza sauce)
  • ​200g Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • ​100g Pepperoni, thinly sliced
  • Dried oregano
  • Parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. ​Prepare the Yeast: Mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Mix the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until the texture is smooth and elastic.
  4. ​First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  5. ​Shape the Pizza: Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually 250°C / 480°F). Punch the dough down to release air and stretch it into a circle on a baking sheet or pizza stone.
  6. Add Toppings: Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough. Sprinkle the mozzarella cheese over the sauce, then arrange the pepperoni slices on top. Sprinkle with a bit of oregano.
  7. ​Bake: Place the pizza in the oven and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly charred.
  8. Serve: Let it cool for a couple of minutes, slice it, and enjoy!
